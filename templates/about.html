System Architecture & Methodology

A full survey of our data pipeline, calibration routines, and SHAP explainability layer.

Project Objective

CrediSense AI is an academic decision-support platform designed to predict credit risk, estimate default probabilities, and generate local SHAP explanations for applicant profiles. It is designed as a university final-year B.Tech machine learning project and is not intended to make real-world lending or loan approval commitments.

Model Details

The core estimator is a tuned **XGBoost Classifier** ensemble model. Features like age and employment duration are cleaned of outliers and duplicates (final training set $N=25,927$ records), stratified by target, and fit inside a pipeline. Variables that are determined downstream (such as `loan_grade` and `loan_int_rate`) are explicitly excluded to align with standard applicant-time workflows.

Probability Calibration

Raw boosting tree scores can be uncalibrated. We apply **Isotonic Calibration** downstream using out-of-fold validation slices. This optimization reduces the Brier Score from 0.1073 to 0.0849, improving probability reliability.

Responsible AI & Fairness

Age can raise fairness, ethical, and regulatory considerations depending on jurisdiction and application context. No claims of legal compliance are made. The system is designed as an educational Decision-Support System (DSS).
